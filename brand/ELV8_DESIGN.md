## BRAND IN ONE LINE

**ELV8 Performance** — a premium, minimalist high-performance coaching brand built around the idea that *high performance isn't an accident.* Visual identity: calm authority. The silence before a big game, not the noise after it.

### Supporting Palette

|------|-----|---------|
| Soft Ivory | `#F7F5EF` | Light backgrounds, long-form PDFs, worksheets, clean carousels, softer editorial pieces |
| Charcoal | `#3A3A3A` | Body copy on light backgrounds, secondary headings, muted contrast |
| Mid Grey | `#8A8A8A` | Secondary text, captions, diagram labels, subtle structure |
| Light Grey | `#D9D9D9` | Dividers, table rules, subtle backgrounds, chart structure |

### Colour Usage Rule (critical)

> **80% black / white / neutral — 15% grey structure — 5% gold accent**

Gold is a punctuation mark, not a colour scheme. Use it to direct the eye to exactly one thing per design. Never fill large areas with gold.

### Colour Psychology

- **Near-Black** → authority, calm, seriousness, depth, premium quality
- **Gold** → excellence, aspiration, earned success, subtle prestige
- **White / Light Neutrals** → clarity, breathing room, reflection, simplicity

## 2. TYPOGRAPHY

### Font Hierarchy *(aligned to elv8.nz, July 2026)*

**Headline Font: Playfair Display** *(locked — this is the hero font)*
- Major headlines (H1/H2): Playfair Display Bold, tight tracking (−0.01em), sentence case
- **Signature move:** one italic accent phrase inside the headline — e.g. "Clear Decisions *When Pressure Rises.*"
- Use for: page/slide titles, section headlines, quote cards, cover pages

**Structure & Body Font: Montserrat** *(locked)*
- Eyebrow labels: Montserrat SemiBold, ~11px, UPPERCASE, very wide tracking (.22em), gold — sits above every major headline ("The Real Risk", "The Outcome")
- Sub-headings (H4): Montserrat SemiBold, uppercase, wide tracking (.08em)
- Body: Montserrat Regular/Medium, 16–18px, generous line height (1.6)
- Buttons/CTAs: Montserrat Bold, ~12px, uppercase, wide tracking (.12em)

**Utility Font: Roboto** *(not primary)*
- Digital dashboards and internal tools only — never brand-facing

### Typography Rules

- The pairing IS the brand: serif gravitas (Playfair) + sans structure (Montserrat). Neither alone.
- Every major section: gold eyebrow label → Playfair headline → Montserrat body. This three-step rhythm is the house pattern.
- One italic accent phrase per headline maximum — it marks the emotional centre of the line
- Left-align body copy; headlines may centre on dark/full-bleed layouts
- Short paragraphs (2–3 lines max on slides)

## 3. LOGO

### Files Available

Located in `ELV8 - Logo /` folder:
- `ELV8.png` — primary logo (dark background use)
- `ELV8 White.png` — reversed for dark backgrounds
- `ELV8 Logo.png`, `ELV8 Logo 2.png`, `ELV8 - logo.png` — variants
- `Logo Banner.png` — horizontal banner format

### Logo Usage Rules

- **Placement:** Small, bottom-right or bottom-centre. Understated, not dominant.
- **Style:** Clean uppercase or refined all-caps, modern sans serif, wide tracking
- **Colour:** White on dark backgrounds; near-black on light backgrounds
- **Gold accent:** Only as a subtle element — never fill the entire logo in gold
- **Feel:** Premium, minimal, quietly confident — not sporty in a cliché way, not heavy

### What the Logo is NOT

Not oversized. Not centred and dominant. Not surrounded by decorative elements. It earns its place at the edge of a design, like a signature.

## 4. VISUAL STYLE PRINCIPLES

### Core Design Language

- **Minimalist** — every element earns its place; meaning over decoration
- **Conceptual** — use metaphor and abstraction, not literal sport imagery
- **Editorial** — think high-end magazine or premium report, not sports poster
- **Premium** — generous white space, clean edges, no clutter
- **Calm authority** — this brand does not shout

### Layout Rules

- Generous negative space (breathing room around all elements)
- Clean edges, no clutter
- Soft, diffused lighting in any imagery
- Thin horizontal gold lines as section dividers (not thick borders)
- Black backgrounds with white centred typography for impact slides
- Light ivory backgrounds for document/worksheet content

### Graphic Elements (recurring)

- Thin gold horizontal rule lines
- Minimal diagrams with clean box/section structure
- Conceptual infographic structures (not data-heavy charts)
- Premium spacing — elements never crowded
- Understated ELV8 wordmark placement

---

## 5. IMAGERY DIRECTION

### Best-fit Imagery Categories

1. **Conceptual metaphors** — compass, layers, pathways, thresholds, tension, balance, direction, foundations, structure
2. **Documentary-style leadership moments** — coaching, team talks, discussion, reflection, sideline moments, quiet intensity
3. **Subtle performance symbolism** — repetition, rhythm, spacing, progression, controlled pressure, alignment

### Athlete Imagery (use sparingly)

When used, athlete imagery must be: subtle, authentic, black and white, emotionally intelligent, not stock-photo

### Imagery to Avoid

- Generic trophy imagery
- Fake hustle / grind visuals
- Obvious motivational clichés ("fist pump" energy)
- Over-saturated sports shots
- Heavy gradients
- Visual noise / crowded backgrounds
- Anything that could appear in a generic "motivational" Instagram account

*Before any design goes out: "Does this feel like something Shea would say standing in front of his team?"*

## 9. IDEAL CLIENT (design audience)

*Full ICP: `ELV8 - Brand/ELV8_ICP.txt`*

- Business leaders who think like coaches
- Coaches across all sports seeking deeper methodology
- Athletes seeking psychological and performance growth
- Age: established professionals (30s–50s typically)
- They have high standards. Generic, cluttered, or try-hard designs will immediately lose trust.
- They respond to: calm confidence, evidence of expertise, clean delivery

**Design implication:** This audience will not be impressed by noise. They are impressed by restraint.

**Gravitas in design:** The visual identity should signal someone who has been in the arena — not someone who coaches from the outside. Restraint is the design equivalent of 320 caps. No decoration that hasn't been earned.

|-------|---------|

| Loud CTAs | Quiet, confident invitations |
| Busy infographics | Minimal, purposeful structure |
| Emoji in branded content | Typography and white space do the work |
| Sports clichés (trophies, fists) | Quiet intensity, documentary-style |
| Corporate colour palettes (blues, greens) | Near-black, ivory, gold only |

## 11. DESIGN QUALITY CHECKLIST

Run this before any design leaves the building:

- [ ] Gold appears in exactly one place per design — not two, not three
- [ ] Logo is small and understated — bottom corner, not centre-stage
- [ ] Headline passes the locker room test — something Shea would actually say
- [ ] Copy is specific, not general — a moment, a number, a real thing
- [ ] Negative space is generous — nothing is crowded
- [ ] No motivational clichés in imagery — no fists, trophies, or grind visuals
- [ ] Type follows the house pairing — Playfair headlines (one italic accent), Montserrat structure/body
- [ ] Colour is 80/15/5 — near-black dominant, grey structure, gold accent only
- [ ] One idea per design — not three ideas competing
- [ ] Would this impress someone with high standards, or just someone easily impressed?

## 13. CSS TOKENS (for HTML / PDF / web outputs)

Use these variables verbatim in any coded output — dashboards, HTML pages, PDF generation, artifacts. No re-interpreting hex values per session.

Verified against the live site's stylesheet (elv8.nz uses these exact variable names and values).

```css
:root {
  /* Colour — matches elv8.nz :root exactly */
  --black:    #222222;  /* backgrounds, dark layouts */
  --gold:     #D4AF37;  /* ONE accent per design */
  --white:    #FFFFFF;  /* headlines on dark */
  --ivory:    #F7F5EF;  /* light backgrounds */
  --charcoal: #3A3A3A;  /* body on light */
  --grey:     #8A8A8A;  /* captions, secondary text */
  --grey-light: #D9D9D9; /* dividers (print/docs) */

  /* Type — Playfair heads, Montserrat structure (as live) */
  --font-head: 'Playfair Display', Georgia, serif;
  --font-sans: 'Montserrat', 'Helvetica Neue', Arial, sans-serif;
  --font-util: 'Roboto', Arial, sans-serif;   /* internal UI only */

  /* Type scale — from live site computed styles */
  --h1: 700 clamp(44px, 6vw, 80px)/1.15 var(--font-head);  /* tracking -0.01em; one <em> italic accent */
  --h2: 700 clamp(32px, 4vw, 51px)/1.15 var(--font-head);  /* tracking -0.01em */
  --eyebrow: 600 11px/1.6 var(--font-sans);  /* UPPERCASE, tracking .22em, color: var(--gold) */
  --h4: 600 17px/1.15 var(--font-sans);      /* UPPERCASE, tracking .08em */
  --body: 400 18px/1.6 var(--font-sans);
  --caption: 500 13px/1.4 var(--font-sans);  /* color: var(--grey) */

  /* CTAs — from live site */
  --cta-primary: 700 12px var(--font-sans);   /* UPPERCASE, tracking .12em,
       color: var(--black), background: var(--gold), padding: 18px 44px, radius 0 */
  --cta-ghost: 600 12px var(--font-sans);     /* UPPERCASE, tracking .12em,
       color: var(--white), border: 1px solid rgba(255,255,255,.3), same padding */

  /* Spacing & structure */
  --space-unit: 8px;
  --pad-card: 40px;                    /* never below 24px */
  --rule: 1px solid var(--gold);       /* thin gold rule, one per design */
  --radius: 0px;                       /* ELV8 is sharp-edged everywhere */
}
```

Enforcement notes: gold appears as the eyebrow label, ONE rule line, or the primary CTA fill — pick one per layout. Primary CTA is the only permitted gold fill. Logo bottom-right at small scale. 80/15/5 colour ratio applies to coded outputs exactly as to static designs.

### House section pattern (from elv8.nz — use in decks, docs, landing pages)

1. Eyebrow: gold, uppercase, wide-tracked Montserrat ("The Real Risk")
2. Headline: Playfair Bold, one italic accent phrase
3. Body: Montserrat, short paragraphs
4. Optional numbered sequence: `01 → 04` steps, gold numbers, Montserrat uppercase titles
5. CTA pair: gold solid + white ghost, side by side, square corners

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE IN CLAUDE DESIGN SESSIONS

Every design prompt should reference this file explicitly and follow this 4-part structure:

```
"Build a [format] for ELV8 Performance. Match DESIGN.md.
Goal: [what this must achieve]
Layout: [structure, sections, responsive rules if applicable]
Content: [actual copy, stats, CTAs to include]
Constraints: [any specific deviations or extra rules for this piece]"
```

**Example:**
> "Build a LinkedIn quote card for ELV8 Performance. Match DESIGN.md. Goal: establish Shea's credibility as someone who has lived elite pressure, not just studied it. Content: 'I didn't want a speech. I didn't want to look on the bright side. I just wanted to sit in the silence.' — Shea McAleese. Constraints: dark background, Playfair Display for the quote, keep it minimal."
