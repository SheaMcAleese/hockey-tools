Pressing tactical reference: the channels that we use and when we're talking about the field.
* Channel 1 is the left side of the ground.
* Right next to that, through where you say it's attacking, the centre channels are Channel 2, Channel 3, Channel 4.
* Channel 5 is the right side of the ground.
That's how we communicate when we're talking to our athletes

Our press structures, so full man to man, are:
* We have everyone on the ground as man marking.
* We have one free defender, so just one free player.
Basically you follow every player wherever they go and avoid them being able to receive and get the ball simply or comfortably. We can also push this into going 1 on 1 all over the ground, which is where our free defender will push up. We play 10 on 10 with the field players one on one. 

We also have our half-court man-to-man, which is nicknamed Helmet. Essentially again we are just man-to-man all over the ground but only in half of the field, allowing the opposition to have the ball freely at the back. We are then going to turn around and press you where we can and just not give you freedom to be able to pass and move around. We will try and step out from our press here 

We then have our half core zonal press which is called Chelsea, which is in reference to the football team, but essentially we are playing half court in the ground. It is our normal zonal defensive structure where we want our back group to be pistoning around the back. The centre back slides underneath and pushes the left half up the ground and to try and defend the pockets of this sideline. This structure is basically pushed around to allow the team to try and breach us down the sidelines in channel 1 and 5, where we can then win the ball in those win zones 

A different press again: we have, in our half-court structure, the box zone. Again in half-court zonal structure we've now created a box in the centre of the ground, so four players working in unison. This is to try and box up the middle of the ground to avoid them coming through the centre and just avoids teams being able to play on angles through our team like they did against the Chelsea press 

And then our last press is the diamond press. Everything is shaped up as a diamond but we're full zone zonally and we are in a full press, so right up the ground with our zonal structures. What you'll end up seeing is these diamonds take shape with the front three, the midfield, the midfield to the back groups. You'll see these consistent diamond shapes and our nickname for that press is Antwerp because obviously Antwerp is the diamond capital of the world
